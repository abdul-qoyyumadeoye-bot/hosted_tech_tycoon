TechTycoon - How to Play

Welcome to TechTycoon

Get ready to become a tech entrepreneur. In this game, you and your team will make tough decisions while running a startup company. You will face budget pressure, user needs, ethics challenges, and unexpected crises. Every choice has consequences.

Your Mission

Build a successful, ethical tech company. You will guide your startup through five to seven critical days, from shaping your vision to launching, managing crises, and scaling responsibly.

Your goal is not just to chase a high score. You are here to learn about:

  • Trade-offs: Speed, quality, inclusion, and money all compete with each other.
  • Real-world complexity: Tech problems rarely have one perfect answer.
  • Ethics: Profitable decisions can still hurt people.
  • Teamwork: Better decisions come from discussion and different perspectives.

How the Game Works

The Setup

  1. Enter your team name and get ready to play.
  2. Choose a problem scenario from the available crisis situations.
  3. Pick your leadership role to frame your approach.
  4. Assemble your team from a range of specialist roles.
  5. Begin the run and work through 5 to 7 decision days.

Each Decision Day

You will read a scenario that explains the challenge your company is facing, then choose from two or three options. Each option affects your scores in a different way.

Example stage: Your team has built a prototype. Do you test it with real users, including people with disabilities, for GBP 6,000, or use automated tests only for GBP 2,000?

Option A: More inclusivity and higher trust, but more spending.
Option B: Faster and cheaper, but more risk of missing real usability issues.

The Four Metrics

Every decision affects four scores. There is no single perfect outcome.

Impact

How effectively did you solve the problem and move the project forward?

Inclusivity

Did you design for a wide range of users and think carefully about fairness and access?

Trust

Did users, partners, and stakeholders feel that your team acted honestly and responsibly?

Budget

How much money do you have left to keep the company moving? If it runs out, the run can end early.

How Decisions Matter

Every choice shifts all four metrics. That is what makes the game interesting.

Hire an accessibility specialist
  • Impact: You make more reliable progress on the real problem.
  • Inclusivity: You take inclusion seriously and improve access.
  • Trust: People see that your team cares.
  • Budget: It costs more money.
Ignore complaints and move forward
  • Impact: You move quickly in the short term.
  • Inclusivity: More users may be excluded.
  • Trust: Stakeholders may lose confidence.
  • Budget: It is cheaper in the moment.

There Is No Single Right Answer

Some teams will prioritise speed. Others will prioritise inclusion or careful budgeting. The important part is discussing the trade-offs and understanding what each decision says about your values.

Best strategy: Talk through every decision with your teammates before choosing. The conversation is where a lot of the learning happens.

Classroom Expectations

During the Game

  • Work as a team: Everyone should have a voice.
  • Be respectful: Different choices come from different priorities.
  • Share the controls: Let different people interact with the game.
  • Play together: Use one device and discuss each stage as a group.
  • No spoilers: Let other teams discover their own outcomes.

After the Game

  • Discuss your decisions: Why did your team choose the path it did?
  • Compare with other teams: What changed when people made different trade-offs?
  • Reflect on ethics: Were there choices that felt uncomfortable?
  • Connect to the real world: How do real companies face similar tensions?

Quick Reference

Game Duration

5 to 15 minutes for a full run.

Team Size

4 to 5 players works best, though one player can also play alone.

What Makes Scenarios Different?

  • Different crisis types, such as accessibility, security, bias, or workplace culture.
  • Different numbers of stages.
  • Different pressures, timings, and trade-offs.
  • Different choices that shape the final outcome.

Key Principles to Remember

  • Everything has a cost: No decision is free.
  • Speed versus quality: Moving fast can create risks.
  • Ethics have value: Trust and inclusion matter.
  • Crises are inevitable: Your response defines your company.
  • Your team is your strength: Diverse perspectives improve decisions.
  • Money matters: Budget carefully if you want to finish the run.

Ready to Play?

Every playthrough is a little different. Make thoughtful decisions, listen to each other, and remember that the goal is not just to finish, but to understand the trade-offs behind responsible tech leadership.

Pro tip: If you want to compare outcomes across a class, your teacher can have everyone focus on the same scenario and then compare how each team responded.
